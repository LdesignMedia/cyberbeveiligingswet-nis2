# Executive Summary: NIS2 / Cyberbeveiligingswet

**Audience:** Management body / Board of Directors (NL).
**Purpose:** Single-page brief satisfying the NIS2 art. 20 governance requirement that the board "gain knowledge sufficient to identify risks and assess management's risk-management practices."
**Reading time:** ~5 minutes.

---

## In one paragraph

The Cyberbeveiligingswet (Cbw) is the Dutch implementation of the EU NIS2 Directive. It expands cybersecurity obligations from a narrow set of "vital" providers to ~18 sectors covering most medium and large entities, makes the **management body personally accountable** for cyber risk-management measures, mandates phased incident reporting (24h / 72h / 1 month) to NCSC-NL, and exposes essential entities to fines up to **€10M or 2% of global turnover**, whichever is higher. The Tweede Kamer adopted the Cbw on 2026-04-15; entry into force is expected ~Q2 2026. Compliance is not optional and is not delegable.

## What the board must do (NIS2 art. 20)

| Duty | Evidence required |
|------|-------------------|
| **Approve** the risk-management measures | Signed, dated board minutes |
| **Oversee** their implementation | Quarterly review on agenda |
| **Complete training** to assess management's practices | Attendance + content + assessment record |

**Personal liability is on the table.** NIS2 art. 20(1) explicitly permits Member States to make management bodies personally liable for breaches.

## Why this is different from what we do today

- **Bigger scope.** Most of our company, not just IT, is in the regulatory perimeter.
- **Board-level liability.** Previous NL regime (Wbni) did not put the board on the hook personally.
- **Sharper timelines.** Significant incidents must be flagged within **24 hours** of awareness.
- **Active supervision.** Essential entities face proactive audits — not just incident-triggered reviews.
- **Supply-chain push-down.** Our suppliers will face our requirements; our customers will face theirs and push them to us.

## What "good" looks like

A defensible posture is built on five visible artefacts the board can point at:

1. **Approved security policy** with management body signature.
2. **Risk register** with prioritised treatments and named owners.
3. **Incident response plan** with tested 24h / 72h / 1mo reporting templates.
4. **Asset + supplier inventory** kept current.
5. **Training records** for board, staff, and high-risk roles.

If we can produce these on demand, we are credible. If we can't, we are not.

## Top three questions the board should ask management

1. **"Are we in scope, and at which tier?"** Essential and important entities have different supervisory regimes and fine caps. Demand a documented answer with the basis (sector + size + jurisdiction).
2. **"Show me the gap."** Ask for a status table: each of the 10 NIS2 art. 21(2) measure areas marked Red / Amber / Green, with named owner and target date.
3. **"What's our 24-hour reporting plan?"** Ask for the named role + decision-tree + dry-run evidence. If management cannot answer in one minute, this is the single biggest exposure.

## Recommended budget signals

(Indicative ranges for a NL medium entity — confirm with management.)

- **Cyber insurance review** to confirm Cbw enforcement coverage.
- **Outside audit / pen-test** (annual) to produce defensible evidence.
- **Board training** (annual; 1–2 hours minimum).
- **MSSP / SOC retainer** if 24/7 detection is not in-house.
- **Tooling top-ups** (MFA, endpoint detection, vulnerability management, SIEM).
- **One named compliance + cyber-risk lead** with budget authority.

A small fraction of revenue spent here is dramatically cheaper than a single significant-incident fine plus reputational damage.

## Recommended motions for the board

1. **Approve** the cybersecurity risk-management measures presented by management as the baseline NIS2 art. 21 implementation.
2. **Schedule** quarterly cyber-risk reviews on the board agenda, starting next session.
3. **Mandate** annual board cybersecurity training; complete first session within 90 days.
4. **Require** management to deliver a Red/Amber/Green gap analysis at the next session.
5. **Request** a tabletop exercise (board + management) within 6 months.
