# NIS2 / Cbw Glossary

Concise definitions for terms used across the NIS2 Directive, the Dutch Cyberbeveiligingswet (Cbw), the Wet weerbaarheid kritieke entiteiten (Wwke), and supporting regulation. Where a term has both an English (EU) and a Dutch (NL) form, both are listed.

Definitions follow the legal source where one exists; otherwise they describe accepted operational use. Cross-references in this file point to the most authoritative article in this corpus.

---

## A

**AMvB (Algemene Maatregel van Bestuur)**
Dutch general administrative order. Lower regulation issued by royal decree. Within this corpus: the *Cyberbeveiligingsbesluit* (under Cbw) and the *Besluit Wwke* (under Wwke).

**Asset (asset, bedrijfsmiddel)**
Information, system, service, or supporting resource of value to an entity. Required to be inventoried under NIS2 art. 21 risk-management measures (asset management).

**Authority — competent authority (bevoegde autoriteit)**
National body designated under NIS2 art. 8 that supervises essential and important entities. NL: the Minister of Justice and Security delegates supervision to sector-specific authorities (RDI for digital infrastructure, IL&T for transport, NVWA for food, etc.).

## B

**BCM / BCP (business continuity management / plan, bedrijfscontinuïteitsbeheer)**
Required under NIS2 art. 21(2)(c). Crisis management, backup, and disaster recovery procedures.

**Besluit Wwke**
Dutch AMvB under the Wwke. Sister regulation to the Cyberbeveiligingsbesluit, covering critical entities under the CER regime.

**Board / management body (bestuur, leidinggevend orgaan)**
Under NIS2 art. 20: must approve risk-management measures, supervise implementation, and follow training. Personal liability for non-compliance is on the table.

## C

**Cbw — Cyberbeveiligingswet**
Dutch primary law transposing NIS2. Wetsvoorstel 36764. Tweede Kamer adopted 2026-04-15. Expected entry into force ~Q2 2026.

**CER Directive — Critical Entities Resilience Directive (EU) 2022/2557**
Sister directive to NIS2 covering physical/organisational resilience of critical entities. NL transposition: Wwke.

**CIR — Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2690**
Binding technical and methodological cybersecurity requirements for digital infrastructure entities (DNS, TLD, cloud, datacenter, CDN, MSP, MSSP, online marketplaces, search engines, social networks, trust services). Adopted 17 October 2024.

**CSIRT — Computer Security Incident Response Team**
Designated incident-response unit per NIS2 art. 10. NL CSIRT: NCSC-NL (for essential entities) + sector CSIRTs.

**Cyberbeveiligingsbesluit (CBB)**
Dutch AMvB under the Cbw. Lower regulation detailing duty of care, registration, board training. Internetconsultatie 20 Feb – 30 March 2025.

## D

**Digital infrastructure (digitale infrastructuur)**
NIS2 Annex I sector covering: internet exchange points, DNS service providers, TLD name registries, cloud computing service providers, datacentre service providers, content delivery network (CDN) providers, trust service providers, public electronic communications networks, and publicly available electronic communications services. Subject to CIR 2024/2690.

**DORA — Digital Operational Resilience Act (EU) 2022/2554**
Financial-sector cyber regulation. Lex specialis: supersedes NIS2 art. 21 + 23 for financial entities.

**DTC — Digital Trust Center**
NL government office providing cybersecurity guidance to non-vital businesses. Source of practical Cbw onboarding material.

**Duty of care (zorgplicht)**
Cbw obligation derived from NIS2 art. 21 — entities must take "appropriate and proportionate" technical, operational, and organisational measures to manage cyber risk. Detailed in the Cyberbeveiligingsbesluit.

## E

**ECSF — European Cybersecurity Skills Framework**
ENISA reference framework mapping cybersecurity job profiles. ENISA's *NIS2 Roles & Skills* publication maps NIS2 obligations to ECSF role profiles.

**eIDAS — Regulation (EU) 910/2014 (and 2024/1183 amending)**
Trust-services regulation. Qualified trust service providers fall under both eIDAS and NIS2 + CIR 2024/2690.

**ENISA — European Union Agency for Cybersecurity**
EU agency producing non-binding technical guidance, including the *Technical Implementation Guidance v1.0* for CIR 2024/2690 and the *Handbook for Cyber Stress Tests*.

**Essential entity (essentiële entiteit)**
NIS2 art. 3(1) tier. Higher-stakes sectors (energy, transport, banking, drinking water, healthcare, digital infrastructure core, public administration, space) and large entities therein. Proactive supervision; max fines €10M or 2% global turnover.

## I

**Important entity (belangrijke entiteit)**
NIS2 art. 3(2) tier. Postal/courier, waste, chemicals, food, manufacturing, digital providers (online marketplaces, search engines, social networks), research. Reactive supervision; max fines €7M or 1.4% global turnover.

**Incident (incident)**
NIS2 art. 6(6) — event compromising the availability, authenticity, integrity, or confidentiality of stored, transmitted, or processed data; or services offered by, or accessible via, network and information systems.

**Incident — significant incident (significant incident)**
NIS2 art. 23(3). An incident that has caused, or is capable of causing, severe operational disruption or financial loss; or has affected, or is capable of affecting, others by causing material/non-material damage. Triggers art. 23 reporting.

**Incident reporting timeline**
NIS2 art. 23: early warning ≤ 24 h, incident notification ≤ 72 h (with assessment), final report ≤ 1 month.

## J

**J&V — Ministerie van Justitie en Veiligheid**
NL Ministry of Justice and Security. Lead ministry for the Cbw + Wwke.

## K

**Kamerstuk**
Tweede Kamer parliamentary document. Format: `<year>D<number>` (e.g. `2025D26060`). Used to cite wetsvoorstellen, amendments, ministerial briefs.

## M

**Management body (leidinggevend orgaan)** — see Board.

**Meldplicht (incident reporting obligation)**
Cbw obligation derived from NIS2 art. 23. Entities must report significant incidents to the CSIRT/competent authority within the phased timelines.

**MSP — Managed Service Provider**
NIS2 Annex II sector. Subject to CIR 2024/2690.

**MSSP — Managed Security Service Provider**
NIS2 Annex II sector. Subject to CIR 2024/2690.

## N

**NCSC-NL — Nationaal Cyber Security Centrum**
NL national CSIRT and lead operational authority for essential entities under the Cbw.

**NIS-CG — NIS Cooperation Group**
EU-level body under NIS2 art. 14 facilitating strategic cooperation and information exchange between Member States.

**NIS1 Directive (EU) 2016/1148**
Predecessor to NIS2, superseded on 18 October 2024.

**NIS2 Directive (EU) 2022/2555**
EU directive on a high common level of cybersecurity. In force 16 January 2023; transposition deadline 17 October 2024.

## R

**Registratieplicht (registration obligation)**
NIS2 art. 27 — entities self-register with the competent authority. NL: registration is via NCSC-NL portal once Cbw enters force.

**Risk-management measures (risicomaatregelen / beheersmaatregelen)**
NIS2 art. 21 — 10 mandatory areas: risk policies; incident handling; BCM/BCP/DR; supply-chain security; security in network/information system acquisition, development, and maintenance; policies and procedures to assess effectiveness; basic cyber hygiene + training; cryptography/encryption; HR security, access control, asset management; MFA, secure communications.

## S

**Supply-chain security (toeleveringsketenbeveiliging)**
NIS2 art. 21(2)(d) — measures addressing direct supplier and service provider relationships. Includes vulnerability handling and provider risk assessment.

## T

**Trust service provider (vertrouwensdienstverlener)**
Per eIDAS art. 3(19). Issuers of qualified certificates and providers of qualified trust services. NIS2 Annex I sector; subject to CIR 2024/2690.

**Tweede Kamer**
Lower house of NL parliament. Adopted Cbw + Wwke on 2026-04-15.

## V

**Vital entity (vitale aanbieder, NL pre-NIS2 term)**
Pre-NIS2 NL designation under the Wbni / Wbni-besluit. Largely subsumed by NIS2 essential-entity status under the Cbw, with carry-over for entities already designated.

## W

**Wbni — Wet beveiliging netwerk- en informatiesystemen**
NL law transposing NIS1. Repealed by the Cbw upon entry into force.

**Wwke — Wet weerbaarheid kritieke entiteiten**
NL primary law transposing the CER Directive (2022/2557). Wetsvoorstel 36765, adopted by Tweede Kamer 2026-04-15.

## Z

**Zorgplicht** — see Duty of care.
